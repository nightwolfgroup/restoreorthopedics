First Session Deposit: We require a $50 deposit to hold your initial evaluation for medical services. This will be applied to the cost of your session at the time of service. Should a late cancellation or no show occur, this will be applied to the associated charge.

Notice of Privacy Practices

In general, the HIPAA privacy rule gives individuals the right to request a restriction on uses and disclosures of their protected health information (PHI). The individual is also provided the right to request confidential communications of PHI be made by alternative means, such as sending correspondence to the individual's office instead of the individual's home.

As a patient, you also have certain rights and responsibilities regarding the use and disclosure of your medical information.

The Law: Keep all individually identifiable health information private, notify you of our legal duties and privacy practices, notify you of any changes in our privacy practices.

Use & Disclosure: your medical and/or personal information will only be disclosed as follows. Unless we receive written and specific authorization from you, we will only use, disclose, or request the minimum amount of protected health information to accomplish the intended purpose.
Treatment - as needed, your medical information can be shared with doctors, nurses, technicians or others directly involved in your care including your current healthcare providers.
Payment - while we may not be directly billing your insurance, should your insurance request information about your care, we can use and disclose your medical information for processing payment. This can also relate to any necessary discussions with attorneys and consulting physicians.
Communication - you have the option of communicating with your practitioner and/or the front desk staff via email. Patient Rights

As a Patient of a Practitioner at the Continuum Consortium, you have the right:

To be treated with courtesy and respect.

To have your privacy protected and to receive our notice of privacy practices.

To have your questions answered promptly.

To know the name, role and qualifications of your practitioner.

To know what services are available, including translators.

To know what rules, apply to you.

To have information about your diagnosis, choices, risks and benefits of treatment so you can assist in developing your plan of care, including the management of pain.

To refuse treatment except as otherwise provided by law.

To be given, on request, information and counseling on available financial resources.

To know, on request and before treatment, weather Medicare assignment is accepted.

To receive, on request and prior to treatment, a reasonable estimate of charges for care and, on request, an itemized bill with charges explained.

To receive treatment regardless of race, national origin, religion, physical handicaps, or sources payment and to expect the appropriate management of pain.

To receive treatment for any emergency medical condition that may get worse if not treated.

To know if medical treatment is for research and to either consent or refuse.

To have the right to make advance directives.

To be free from restraint and seclusion which are not medically necessary.

To the confidentiality of your medical record and the right to access information from it.

To have a family member or representative and/or physician notified promptly of admission to the hospital.

As a Patient of a Practitioner at the Continuum Consortium, you are responsible:

To give your practitioner correct, complete information about your present medical condition, past illnesses, hospitalizations, medications, including over the counter drugs/herbal supplements, and other health matters.

To report changes in your condition and report perceived safety concerns in your care.

To tell your practitioner if you understand the plan of treatment and what is expected of you, including pain relief options and ask questions if you do not understand.

To follow the treatment plan recommended by your healthcare provider.

To keep appointments or notify the practitioner or facility if you cannot.

To accept responsibility for your actions if you refuse treatment or do not follow the healthcare providers instructions.

To meet your healthcare financial obligations promptly.

To follow rules and regulations on patient care.

To be considerate of the Consortium's personnel and property.
